Course Introduction

  • Course Number: 04832250
  • Time: Monday, 10:10am-12am; Thursday, 1pm-2:50pm
  • Location: Science Teaching Building, classroom # to be assigned
  • Course description: This is an undergraduate level course on computer networks. The course provides an overview of fundamental networking theories as well as emerging technologies in the future computer networks, including: Internet, computer networking protocol stack, wireless networks, mobile Internet, network security, sensor networks, cutting-edge networking technologies, etc. Besides these materials, this course offers plenty of great opportunities for students to work with our guest lecturers—senior engineers in the Internet industry—who will serve as student mentors for the course projects. Note that this course will be given in English. The students will receive a systematic study of computer networks, along with detailed technical guidance on their projects.
